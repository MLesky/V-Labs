Exploring Center and Variability

Here are the steps to perform the Experiment on Exploring Center and Variability

    Aim:To investigate the concepts of center and variability in mathematics using dice rolls.

    Materials:Two dice, Paper and pencil

    Procedures

  1. Roll the two dice ten times and record the sum of the numbers rolled each time.
    Calculate the mean (average) of the ten sums by adding them together and dividing by ten.
    Calculate the median of the ten sums by arranging them in order from smallest to largest and finding the middle value.
    Calculate the range of the ten sums by subtracting the smallest sum from the largest sum.
    Repeat the experiment with 20, 30, and 40 rolls and record the mean, median, and range for each set of rolls.
    Compare the results for each set of rolls and observe how the mean, median, and range change as the number of rolls increases.

    2. Observation

    As the number of rolls increases, the mean and median tend to converge towards the true average of the dice rolls (7), while the range tends to decrease.
