Experimental Probability of Rolling a Number on a Die

Here are the steps to perform the Experiment on Experimental Probability of Rolling a Number on a Die

    Aim:To investigate the probability of rolling a specific number on a die using experimental methods.

    Materials:One six-sided die, Paper and pencil

    Procedures

  1. Roll the die 20 times and record the number that comes up each time.
    Count the number of times the specific number you are interested in (e.g. the number 3) comes up.
    Calculate the experimental probability of rolling the specific number by dividing the number of times it came up by the total number of rolls (20).
    Repeat the experiment with the same number of rolls, and calculate the experimental probability for each number on the die.
    Compare the results for each number and observe how they relate to the theoretical probability of rolling each number on a six-sided die..

    2. Observation

    The experimental probability of rolling a specific number on a die should be close to the theoretical probability, which is 1/6 or approximately 0.167.
