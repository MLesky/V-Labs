States of Matter

Here are the steps to perform the Experiment on the States of Matter:

    Aim:o investigate the three states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) and observe how they can change from one state to another.

    Materials:Ice cubes Water, Heat source (e.g. hot plate or stove), Glass beaker, Thermometer, Measuring cup, Plastic bag

    Procedures

  1. Fill a glass beaker with water and place a thermometer in the water to measure the temperature.
  2. Place a few ice cubes in the water and observe what happens to the ice as it comes into contact with the water.
  3. Heat the beaker on a heat source until the water begins to boil. Observe the steam that is produced as the water changes from a liquid to a gas
  4. Pour some of the water into a plastic bag and seal the bag tightly. Place the bag in the freezer and observe what happens to the water as it changes from a liquid to a solid.

    5. Observation

    When the ice cubes are added to the water, they begin to melt and turn into liquid water. As the water is heated, it begins to boil and produce steam as it changes from a liquid to a gas. When the water in the plastic bag is frozen, it turns into a solid block of ice.
